<h1>Common Quality Assurance (QA) Team Guidelines</h1>

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="highlight">Introduction</span> to QA Team Guidelines</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>
A <span class="highlight">Quality Assurance (QA) Team</span> plays an essential <span class="highlight">role</span> in ensuring that products meet defined standards before they reach customers. Common QA guidelines help align team efforts, promote best practices, and maintain consistency across projects. This documentation details the pivotal rules and expectations for QA team members, ensuring a unified and effective approach.
 [autosync-test-0720] [AUTOSYNC-LIVE2-1148]</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="highlight">Role</span> of the QA Team</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>
The <span class="highlight">QA team</span> is dedicated to verifying product quality through systematic testing and process optimization. Their responsibilities extend beyond bug finding to ensuring comprehensive coverage and preventing future issues. Key roles and best practices include:
</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><br>    <strong>Requirement Analysis:</strong> Understanding the product features and project scope for effective test planning.<br>  </li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><br>    <strong>Test Planning and Design:</strong> Crafting detailed <span class="highlight">test cases</span>, test scenarios, and deciding on optimal test methodologies.<br>  </li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><br>    <strong>Test Execution:</strong> Performing functional, integration, regression, and acceptance tests.<br>  </li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><br>    <strong>Defect Reporting:</strong> Logging defects with clarity and precision using standardized ticket formats. For more about issue tracking, see <a href="https://www.atlassian.com/software/jira">JIRA documentation</a>.<br>  </li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><br>    <strong>Collaboration:</strong> Working closely with developers, product managers, and other stakeholders to address issues and improve processes.<br>  </li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><br>    <strong>Process Improvement:</strong> Identifying gaps and suggesting enhancements to QA processes and tools.<br>  </li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Core <span class="highlight">QA Guidelines</span></h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>
The following guidelines standardize QA activities and outcomes:
</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:table -->
<figure class="wp-block-table"><table class="has-fixed-layout"><thead><tr><th>Guideline</th><th>Description</th><th>Example</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td>Documentation</td><td>Create clear, concise, and version-controlled test documentation.</td><td>Maintain test case repositories in tools such as <a href="https://www.qtestnet.com/">qTest</a>.</td></tr><tr><td>Reproducibility</td><td>Ensure all reported bugs can be reproduced using provided steps and data.</td><td>
        <pre><code>Steps to reproduce:
1. Log in as a standard user.
2. Navigate to the settings page.
3. Click 'Save' without changing any field.
Expected outcome: Success message.
Actual outcome: Error message displayed.</code></pre>
      </td></tr><tr><td>Traceability</td><td>Map all test cases back to their corresponding requirements.</td><td>Traceability matrices linking requirements to test cases.</td></tr><tr><td>Communication</td><td>Consistent and timely communication of issues to relevant teams.</td><td>Daily stand-up updates and regular bug triage meetings.</td></tr><tr><td>Continuous Learning</td><td>Participate in training and stay updated with latest QA trends and tools.</td><td>
        Attend webinars such as <a href="https://testguild.com/webinars/">TestGuild QA Webinars</a>.
      </td></tr></tbody></table></figure>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="highlight">Sample Defect Report Format</span></h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>
A standardized defect report helps streamline issue resolution:
</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:code -->
<pre class="wp-block-code"><code>Summary: Brief description of the bug
Environment: Application version, OS, Browser
Steps to Reproduce:
1. First step
2. Second step
Expected Result: What should happen
Actual Result: What actually happens
Attachments: Screenshots, logs (if available)
Priority/Severity: Indicate the level of impact</code></pre>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><span class="highlight">Conclusion</span></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
