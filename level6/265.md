<h1>You can get up to Tk40 lakh personal loan, Tk80 lakh for electric cars</h1>

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Previous limit was Tk20 lakh.</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><img width="451" height="254" alt="Illustration: Ashrafun Naher Ananna/TBS Creative" src="https://www.tbsnews.net/sites/default/files/styles/big_2/public/images/2026/02/15/electric-cars.png"></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Illustration: Ashrafun Naher Ananna/TBS Creative</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>An individual will now be able to take a maximum loan of Tk40 lakh from a bank, up from the previous limit of Tk20 lakh. Bangladesh Bank has also allowed higher loan limits for purchasing electric vehicles.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The central bank issued a circular on Tuesday (5 May) and sent it to all commercial banks.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>According to the circular, an individual can receive a personal loan of up to Tk40 lakh. Consumer lending rules have also been revised in consideration of prevailing market prices and rising demand for consumer goods.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":4} -->
<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><a href="https://news.google.com/publications/CAAqBwgKMIivlQsw2ZKrAw?ceid=US:en&amp;oc=3">&nbsp;Keep updated, follow The Business Standard's Google news channel</a></h4>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Under the new circular, an individual can take a maximum auto loan of Tk60 lakh for conventional vehicles. However, for electric and hybrid vehicles, the limit has been raised to Tk80 lakh.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><a href="https://www.tbsnews.net/bangladesh/govt-suspends-interest-free-car-loans-officials-1401701"></a></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><a href="https://www.tbsnews.net/bangladesh/govt-suspends-interest-free-car-loans-officials-1401701">Govt suspends interest-free car loans for officials</a></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The circular said new facilities had been introduced in auto loans to increase the use of environmentally friendly vehicles in the country.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The debt-equity ratio has also been revised. For conventional vehicles, loans can be provided at a maximum ratio of 60:40, while for electric and hybrid vehicles the ratio has been increased to 80:20.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>It also said that when determining a person's total loan exposure, auto loans taken by dependent family members would also be included.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The circular further said banks must distribute consumer loans in such a way that loan growth in the consumer financing sector does not exceed the bank's overall loan growth.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><a href="https://www.tbsnews.net/economy/cabinet-approves-tax-relief-brand-new-electric-vehicle-imports-1428671"></a></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><a href="https://www.tbsnews.net/economy/cabinet-approves-tax-relief-brand-new-electric-vehicle-imports-1428671">Cabinet approves tax relief for brand new electric vehicle imports</a></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Previous directives issued in 2004, 2017 and 2024 on the matter have been cancelled through the new circular.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Bangladesh Bank issued the instruction under Section 45 of the Bank Companies Act, 1991, and it takes immediate effect.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->
