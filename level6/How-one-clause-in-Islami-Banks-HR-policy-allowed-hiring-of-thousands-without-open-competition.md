<h1>How one clause in Islami Bank&#039;s HR policy allowed hiring of thousands without open competition</h1>

<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><img width="452" height="254" alt="The logo of Islami Bank PLC. Photo: Collected" src="https://www.tbsnews.net/sites/default/files/styles/big_2/public/images/2026/03/16/islami_bank.jpg"></p>

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The logo of Islami Bank PLC. Photo: Collected</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Thousands of officers were recruited to Islami Bank Bangladesh during the seven years S Alam Group controlled it – without national newspaper advertisements, written tests, or, in many cases, even oral interviews – under the Awami League regime.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>From 2017, when S Alam took control of the bank, until August 2024, a total of 10,832 officers were recruited, according to reports by Bangladesh Bank and four audit firms. Of them, 8,542 were appointed without any advertisement in national dailies.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The bank's recruitment policy was amended to legitimise the process.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":4} -->
<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><a href="https://news.google.com/publications/CAAqBwgKMIivlQsw2ZKrAw?ceid=US:en&amp;oc=3">&nbsp;Keep updated, follow The Business Standard's Google news channel</a></h4>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Policy clause that opened floodgates</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Audit reports say the bank amended its human resources policy to legitimise the recruitment process. Clause 7.04 of the policy stated, "In case of exigency, the Managing Director &amp; CEO of the Bank may appoint manpower…relaxing the above recruitment formalities."</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Using this clause, regular recruitment practices – such as public advertisements, written tests, and competitive selection – were largely ignored.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Instead, an informal system was introduced to collect CVs.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Boxes labelled "General," "Cash," "Male," "Female," "Diploma," and other categories were reportedly placed at the group chairman's residence in Patiya and an office in Asadganj, Chattogram.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Applicants submitted CVs into these boxes, from where selected applications were sent to the bank's head office.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Appointment letters were then issued directly according to prepared lists, without open competition.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Audit sources described the system as a "closed recruitment loop," making participation by outside candidates virtually impossible.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->
