<h1>Govt increases fuel supply from today as chaos lingers on</h1>

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Energy Minister Iqbal Hasan Mahmud Tuku blamed panic buying, stockpiling and black market activity for the shortages at filling stations</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->

<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>A day after hiking fuel prices, the government has announced a 10-20% increase in diesel, octane and petrol supplies from today to ease the shortages that kept motorists waiting in long queues for hours in Dhaka and elsewhere as of yesterday.</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><img width="1600" height="181" alt="Highlights: TBS" src="https://tbsnews.net/sites/default/files/styles/infograph/public/images/2026/04/20/p1-hi11111.jpg"></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Highlights: TBS</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>In a notification last night, the Energy Division said the distribution companies under the Bangladesh Petroleum Corporation (BPC) will sell 13,048 tonnes of diesel, 1,422 tonnes of octane and 1,511 tonnes of petrol per day from 20 April. This marks a 10% increase for diesel and petrol, and 20% for octane.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:image -->
<figure class="wp-block-image"><img src="https://tbsnews.net/sites/default/files/styles/infograph/public/images/2026/04/19/fuel-supply-govt-order-19april2026.jpeg" alt="" title=""/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>"Considering the present demand for fuel oils, companies under the BPC have been instructed to sell at the increased rates to keep up the supplies at dealers' and consumers' levels," reads the notification.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":4} -->
<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><a href="https://news.google.com/publications/CAAqBwgKMIivlQsw2ZKrAw?ceid=US:en&amp;oc=3">&nbsp;Keep updated, follow The Business Standard's Google news channel</a></h4>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The government raised the price of octane by Tk20 per litre, petrol by Tk19 and diesel by Tk15, effective from yesterday.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><a href="https://www.tbsnews.net/bangladesh/energy/no-real-fuel-shortage-bangladesh-crisis-artificially-created-energy-minister"></a></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><a href="https://www.tbsnews.net/bangladesh/energy/no-real-fuel-shortage-bangladesh-crisis-artificially-created-energy-minister">No real fuel shortage in Bangladesh, crisis artificially created: Energy minister</a></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Earlier in parliament yesterday, Energy and Mineral Resources Minister Iqbal Hasan Mahmud Tuku blamed panic buying, stockpiling and black market activity for the shortages at filling stations.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>He said there is no shortage of fuel in the country, but that an artificial crisis is being created.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Despite a sharp increase in fuel prices aimed at reducing the subsidy burden, motorists across Bangladesh continued to face long queues at filling stations and shortages yesterday, with no sign of an improvement in supply.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Many motorists had expected the price increase to be followed by higher supplies from depots to petrol stations, easing the shortages that have persisted for weeks. Instead, the situation remained largely unchanged.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Drivers and motorcyclists in Dhaka and elsewhere in the country were still waiting for hours at filling stations to obtain fuel.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><a href="https://www.tbsnews.net/bangladesh/energy/fuel-crisis-persists-gazipur-despite-price-hike-1415801"></a></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><a href="https://www.tbsnews.net/bangladesh/energy/fuel-crisis-persists-gazipur-despite-price-hike-1415801">Fuel crisis persists in Gazipur despite price hike</a></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>At some filling stations, motorcyclists who joined queues at midnight on Saturday were only able to buy fuel yesterday afternoon. Even then, some said they had not been allowed to fill their tanks.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Measures taken by the Energy Division to tackle hoarding and black market sales, including appointing tag officers at filling stations, forming monitoring teams at district and upazila level and conducting mobile court operations, have so far failed to improve the situation.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Energy Adviser to the Consumers Association of Bangladesh (CAB) M Shamsul Alam said the ministry had created a narrative that "fuel reserves are overflowing and that there is insufficient storage space".</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>"Yet people are standing on the streets for hours without getting fuel. The Energy Division, the BPC, the Competition Commission, the Directorate of National Consumer Rights Protection and the law enforcement agencies are all standing like wooden puppets. There are no words to describe this," he told TBS.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Shamsul Alam said the public could have accepted the difficulties if the government had acknowledged that supply is being disrupted by the Middle East conflict, the dollar shortage and uncertainty over the Strait of Hormuz.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>"Instead, the ministry has created the opposite narrative and is not even admitting there is a fuel shortage. That is proof of the government's serious inefficiency and inability to control the supply chain," he said.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Asked whether fuel supplies would be increased after the price rise, Energy and Mineral Resources Division spokesman Monir Hossain Chowdhury said, "We are often supplying more than the allocated amount. But unless panic buying stops, the situation will not return to normal."</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Sajjadul Karim Kabul, president of one faction of the Petrol Pump Owners' Association, said depot supplies had not increased despite the higher prices.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>"What is the point of raising fuel prices? The crisis will not end unless supplies increase. If they supplied fuel at full capacity for one week, everything would calm down," he said.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Kabul said officials appear worried that the Strait of Hormuz could be closed again and are therefore reluctant to release more fuel.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->
